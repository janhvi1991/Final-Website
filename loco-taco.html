Project Vision


Loco Taco is a food-ordering app, designed to help users track, customize, and order food easily from a food truck.



This is a personal project, done single-handedly, to holistically understand UX/UI design principles, technicalities, and interactions.

Role

  1. Product Design
  2. Product Strategy
  3. User Research
  4. Information Architecture
  5. Interaction
  6. Visual Design
  7. Prototyping & Testing

Challenges

  1. Incorporate a way for users to customize their orders easily.
  2. Create an interface that allows users to quickly and easily place an order.

The Starting Point


I designed this project as a part of the Google UX Design Professional Certificate, in order to holistically understand the UX design process.

I have used apps to order food online ever since they have been in existence. I love that I can take my time to thoroughly look through the menu, and don't have to give my order to another human being on the phone.

Apart from network issues and disturbances, you can just never be sure that you were clear enough with your instructions, or that the person on the other end could understand you. Along with that, you have to make sure that you've got everyone's order with all their special instructions or you're going to be awkwardly going back and forth with your friends or family while you're on the phone. It's all very stressful, especially for introverts like me.

The Problem


Look, ordering food on the phone is just not convenient, okay?

But I think there are certain other issues with ordering food online.


The Solution


LOCO TACO is an app for a food-truck serving authentic Mexican food.

The menu is small but customizable, and because the truck parks in different locations, it can deliver to a wider range of localities.


The Research


While ordering food online is not a novel idea, I began thinking specifically of mobile restaurants, like a food truck.

I remember back in 2013, a lady in Mumbai started selling cookies out of her car. It blew up, partly because her cookies were fantastic, and partly because they were in such short supply. Everyone wanted a taste, and you could never guess where she would pop up next. It was a fantastic strategy.

Similarly, I loved the idea of a food-truck selling limited portions of authentic Mexican food. I dug a little deeper into why this idea was appealing to me.

Style-Guide



Using a warm and energizing palette to give the user a sense of comfort and excitement was important. I used shades of blue and green, with a pop of yellow to keep things interesting.

The main typeface of choice was Josefins Sans, a clean sans serif font. I paired this with the hand-drawn Amatic SC to add a dash of playfulness.


Prototype



I thought that it was important for the user to view the menu as soon as they got onto the app. Therefore I incorporated the menu into the homepage. I also wanted users to easily locate the food-truck or customize their food, once they were on the app.

    • Takeaways



    Loco Taco made me realize that there is more to an app than just visual design.
